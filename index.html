technical HCI researcher and software engineer

i’m a computer scientist and senior at Brown University, where my research is advised by Professor Jeff Huang at the Human-Computer Interaction Lab. i’m the main developer and a Research Assistant for Self-E, a mobile user system that leverages remotely-captured user data to enable self-discovery.

my interests span personal informatics, the politics of data, secure communication systems, social media, surveillance studies, and critical infrastructure studies. my long-term goal is to distill insights from feminist sts, critical race, and media infrastructure studies to build technical systems that are innovative, accountable, and sensitive to the human experience. as a researcher i’m driven by the notion of “studying up.”

this spring I will also be TAing CS for Social Good, where we will engage in some of the ethical considerations that arise within the field of computing while also delivering a final projects in partnership with local non-profits. i have previously TA’d the introductory CS sequence (CS15 and CS16).

this past summer i had a fascinating and eye-opening experience interning at Facebook in the machine learning infrastructure org.

i’m pretty much always happy to grab a cup of tea. in my free time i like to cook, read, and make collages. see my resume, or my CV
